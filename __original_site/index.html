The Kinneret Council on Aging focuses on serving the needs of those seniors residing in the Kinneret Apartment community of downtown Orlando, FL. Providing them with amenities, social activities, and programming to better the lives of those that cared for them. Wanting to amplify their message of giving and responsibility, the KCOA wanted their website to reflect their message without distraction.

Needing to update their brand, as well as making sure the information they wanted to get out there was being received, the KCOA asked for a clean site that was built following the fundamentals of ease and simplicity. Based on an analysis of their previous website, it was recommended they prioritize the information they wanted to convey in order to improve repeat visits and overall site usage.

The KCOA wanted very much for their brand to be on par with related organizations that operated in other communities nationwide. In order for their site to be a source of information they could point current and potential residents to, it was imperative that the content of the website was streamlined and lean.

Planning was crucial, and overhauling the site’s page structure was key - sorting the content into sensible sections, and creating a site structure document was the first step in that course. There was a lot of talk and discussion before moving onto creating the first design iterations.

Through a lot of internal and vendor/client discussions, the KCOA knew what they needed were: clear headings that held apartment application information, a detailed mission statement, and most importantly a straightforward gateway for donations to be made.

Going through multiple iterations of revisions, the KCOA’s needs were what guided the design process. Working with the KCOA closely was crucial in assuring that the designs being created were up to their standards, and on par with their passion for what they do.

To ensure a quality product that everybody was satisfied with, communication was key. By working closely with the client, it became apparent that a very important aspect of the redesign, was the payment gateway for their donations. Given that they are a non-profit, they rely heavily on donations to cover their operational costs. A custom form that forwarded information over HTTPS to a PayPal checkout, was used to integrate with their already existing donation accounts.

“We are thrilled to launch our new website which has a new look and feel,” said Sharon F. Weil, director of Programming and Development, KCOA. “The website is clean, bright and much more easily navigated.”

Jessica Keller is a cinematographer and photographer based in Orlando, Florida. Always chasing the human element, she knows the power of storytelling and strives to capture images that speak. Jessica has worked on features and documentaries, as well as brand films and commercials for Fortune 500 brands. Needing to highlight her credentials in a more customizeable fashion than a site builder could do.

With aesthetic being the drive of the re-design, a custom Wordpress theme was created that was heavily inspired by the magazine layouts of Nylon. Minimalism, lots of negative space and her work being the focus of the users attention - no distractions. The work needed to be unobstructed and it's space needed to be distraction-free.
